History

TechCon began in 2010 as a small gathering of 50 passionate developers in a community center in Austin, Texas. What started as a weekend meetup has evolved into one of the premier technology conferences in North America, attracting over 10,000 attendees annually.

The conference was founded by three software engineers who wanted to create a platform where technology enthusiasts could share knowledge, network, and inspire innovation. Over the years, TechCon has witnessed the rise of mobile computing, cloud technologies, artificial intelligence, and blockchain.

From our humble beginnings, we have hosted groundbreaking announcements, product launches, and technical breakthroughs that have shaped the industry. Notable moments include the first public demonstration of several now-ubiquitous technologies and frameworks that millions of developers use today.

Key Milestones

  • 2010: First TechCon with 50 attendees
  • 2013: Expanded to 500 participants and introduced workshops
  • 2016: Reached 2,000 attendees and added virtual participation
  • 2019: Celebrated our first international speakers
  • 2022: Hybrid format introduced post-pandemic
  • 2024: 15th anniversary celebration with record attendance

Mission

Our mission is to foster innovation, collaboration, and knowledge sharing within the global technology community. We believe that bringing together diverse voices from different backgrounds, industries, and experience levels creates an environment where breakthrough ideas can flourish.

Core Values

TechCon 2024 is built on four fundamental principles that guide everything we do:

Innovation

We showcase cutting-edge technologies and encourage attendees to think beyond current limitations. Our conference serves as a launchpad for new ideas and technological breakthroughs.

Inclusivity

Technology belongs to everyone. We are committed to creating an inclusive environment that welcomes participants regardless of their background, experience level, gender, race, or geographic location.

Education

Learning never stops in technology. Our comprehensive program includes beginner-friendly sessions, advanced technical deep-dives, and everything in between.

Community

The relationships formed at TechCon extend far beyond the conference dates. We facilitate networking opportunities and maintain year-round community engagement through our online platforms.

Past Speakers

Over the years, TechCon has been honored to host some of the most influential figures in technology. Our speakers have included startup founders, industry veterans, open-source contributors, and visionary leaders who have shaped the digital landscape.

Featured Alumni Speakers

Dr. Sarah Chen

Chief AI Officer, InnovateAI

Dr. Chen keynoted TechCon 2023 with her groundbreaking presentation on ethical AI development. With over 15 years of experience in machine learning research, she has published 50+ papers and holds 12 patents in neural network optimization. Her work on bias detection in AI systems has influenced industry standards worldwide.

Marcus Rodriguez

Founder & CEO, CloudScale Technologies

Marcus shared his journey from solo developer to leading a $2B cloud infrastructure company at TechCon 2022. His session on "Scaling from Zero to Millions" became our most-watched presentation. He's known for his advocacy of sustainable computing and has revolutionized energy-efficient data center design.

Dr. Aisha Patel

Director of Cybersecurity Research, SecureNet Institute

A cybersecurity pioneer who has protected critical infrastructure for over two decades. Dr. Patel's TechCon 2021 presentation on quantum-resistant encryption helped prepare the industry for post-quantum cryptography. She has advised governments and Fortune 500 companies on digital security strategies.

Jamie Thompson

Lead Developer, Open Source Initiative

Jamie has contributed to over 100 open-source projects and maintains several widely-used JavaScript frameworks. Their TechCon 2020 workshop on "Building Inclusive Development Communities" established best practices now adopted by major tech companies. They're passionate about making technology accessible to underrepresented groups.
